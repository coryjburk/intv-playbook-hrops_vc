01 Getting started

The playbook is a single self-contained web page — nothing to install and no account to create. Open it in your browser and everything runs locally. It is built around 48 HR Operations interview questions across eight categories, with a model answer, a deeper rationale, coaching notes, and the red flags to avoid for each.

A typical loop: browse or search the question bank, read a question's layered answers, practice your own answer out loud in the voice studio, generate an AI-coach prompt to get graded, then log the scores so the tool can track your readiness over time.

Tip

Practice out loud, not just by reading. Interviewers grade your reasoning and delivery, and saying an answer aloud surfaces gaps that silent reading hides.

02 The question bank

The Question Bank is the core of the playbook. Each question expands to four layers:

  • Model answer — a strong, natural spoken response you could give.
  • Deep dive — the fuller rationale and what a top answer covers, for understanding rather than memorizing.
  • Coaching — how to score points and what differentiates a strong answer.
  • Red flag — the mistakes that sink candidates on that question.

Finding questions

  • Search — type any keyword to filter across questions and answers.
  • Filter by category (HR Ops & Service, HRIS & Data, Compliance & Risk, Process Improvement, Analytics & Metrics, Lifecycle & Experience, Stakeholder & Leadership, Behavioral) or by difficulty (Foundational, Core, Advanced).

Tracking your progress

  • Mark a question reviewed once you've worked through it — the reviewed count updates as you go.
  • Set a confidence rating on each question so you can come back to the weak spots.
Good to know

Your reviewed marks and confidence ratings are saved automatically in this browser (see Your data & privacy). They persist between sessions on the same device and browser.

03 Models, battlecard & red flags

Three reference sections back up the question bank:

  • Models & Frameworks — eight models interviewers expect you to reach for (Employee Lifecycle, Tiered Service Delivery, SIPOC, DMAIC, RACI, the Ulrich operating model, Moments That Matter, and Root Cause Analysis). Each lists when to use it, the steps, and a tip. Name them naturally in answers, never robotically.
  • Battlecard — an eight-section quick-reference: must-know concepts, power phrases, metrics to know cold, process principles, common mistakes, strong-candidate signals, senior-round differentiators, and a last-minute review.
  • Red Flags — ten patterns that weaken candidates, each with the warning signals and how to fix them.
Tip

The night before an interview, the Last-Minute Review battlecard section and the Red Flags are the highest-leverage things to skim.

04 Readiness & how scoring works

The playbook grades your answers on a six-dimension rubric: Process & Operational Rigor, Compliance & Risk Awareness, Stakeholder & Employee Insight, Data & Metrics, Judgment in Ambiguity, and Communication & Influence.

When your AI coach grades an answer (next section), you log the 1–5 score it gave on each dimension. The tool averages those into an overall score out of 100 and maps it to a readiness tier — from Getting Started through Developing, Competitive, Interview Ready, and Offer Ready — and tracks your trend across sessions.

How to read it

The tiers are a self-coaching signal, not a guarantee. They reflect the scores you log, so they're only as honest as your inputs. Use the trend to see where you're improving and which dimensions need work — not as a verdict.

05 Voice practice studio

The Voice Practice Studio lets you answer a question out loud while the tool transcribes you and reports your word count, duration, speaking pace (words per minute), and a rough filler-word tally. It's designed to build the muscle of answering aloud and to flag pacing problems (too fast, too slow) and obvious rambling.

Using it

  1. Open a question and start the practice studio.
  2. Click Start speaking and answer as if the interviewer is across the table.
  3. Watch your live stats; click stop when done.
  4. Reveal the model answer to compare, then generate an AI-coach prompt (next section).

Reading your transcript — important

The transcript is produced by your browser's built-in speech recognition, not by the playbook and not by a recording of your voice. That engine is good at everyday words but has two real limits you should expect:

  • It mangles acronyms and jargon. Terms like “HRIS,” “HRBP,” “FLSA,” and “COBRA” aren't in its everyday vocabulary, so it substitutes the nearest familiar words.
  • It drops or rewrites filler words. Speech engines are tuned to produce clean text, so “um” and “uh” are often deleted or turned into a real word like “I'm.”
You say: “An HRIS is the core system…”
Browser hears: “finish your ass is the core system…”
What this means for you

This is a limitation of the free browser engine, not a sign that you spoke unclearly and not a defect in the playbook. Speaking more slowly helps a little with everyday words, but it won't make the engine learn “HRIS” or reliably keep your “um”s.

So use the transcript for what it's good at: your pace, your structure, whether you rambled or stayed tight, and roughly how long you spoke. Don't judge yourself on word-perfect spelling.

Treat the filler-word count as a floor, not a precise measure. Because the engine drops fillers, a count of zero doesn't prove you were crisp — it may just mean the engine swallowed them. Trust your own ear for fillers, and let the AI coach assess your actual delivery.

A more accurate transcript — one that recognizes HR terminology and keeps your fillers — is on the roadmap, but it requires routing audio to a cloud speech service rather than the in-browser engine.

06 AI coach & rubric logging

The playbook doesn't grade your spoken answer itself — instead it generates a detailed prompt you paste into an AI assistant (such as Claude) to get a realistic mock interview or a critique. The prompt already includes the question, the evaluation criteria, and — in the practice studio — your transcribed answer.

Two ways to use it

  • From the AI Coach section — pick a category and a mode (for example, a mock interview or a critique) and copy the generated prompt.
  • From the practice studio — after answering out loud, copy the prompt that bundles your answer with the grading rubric.

Logging your scores

When the AI coach returns a score on each rubric dimension, tap the 1–5 value it gave for each. The tool rolls them into your overall score and readiness tier and updates your trend (see Readiness).

Tip

Because the transcript can garble acronyms, glance at it before sending the prompt. If a key term came out wrong, fix it in the pasted text so the AI grades what you actually said.

07 Your data & privacy

Everything you do in the playbook — reviewed marks, confidence ratings, and logged rubric scores — is stored locally in your own browser using its built-in storage. It is not uploaded anywhere by the playbook.

  • Your progress is tied to the specific browser and device you use. Switching browsers or devices, or clearing your browser data, starts you fresh.
  • Voice practice is transcribed by the browser; the playbook keeps your transcript only on the page while you work and doesn't store recordings.
  • When you use the AI coach, you choose what to paste into the assistant — that step is between you and that tool.

08 Requirements & troubleshooting

Browser

  • Use a recent desktop Chrome (or another Chromium-based browser) for the best experience, especially the voice studio — in-browser speech recognition is most reliable there.
  • If the voice studio says speech isn't supported, your browser likely lacks the speech-recognition feature; switch to Chrome.

Microphone

  • The first time you start the voice studio, allow microphone access when prompted.
  • If you accidentally blocked it, re-enable mic permission for the page in your browser's site settings, then reload.

My progress disappeared

  • Progress is per browser and device. If it's gone, you're likely on a different browser/device, in a private/incognito window, or your browser data was cleared.

The transcript is wrong

  • Expected for acronyms and fillers — see Voice practice studio. It's the browser engine, not your delivery. Judge yourself on pace and structure, not spelling.
